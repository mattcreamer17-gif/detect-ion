A note for Detect-ION

Real-time detection is a hardware problem. Reproducible detection across sites and revisions is a context problem.

CLARION beta went up in February. The Mayo Florida Pseudomonas collaboration landed in December. A short read on where the next eighteen months get harder, and where that work sits.

Matt Creamer, CRO Eduba Thirty minutes

You miniaturized complex mass spectrometry to chip scale. CLARION's beta rollout, the Mayo Clinic Florida collaboration on Pseudomonas aeruginosa, the Moffitt Cancer Center VOC study, the MATTHIAS project across the Republic of Congo: the scientific foundation is already there. The next eighteen months are a different kind of problem. You need the hardware revisions, the clinical sites, the analyte libraries, and the ML classifier to hold a single, auditable story. One an FDA reviewer can reconstruct. One a Series A diligence partner can follow. One a DoD program officer can verify.

This is where we work. Eduba designs the context architecture that sits above the instrument and below the model, so the evidence chain stays legible as each layer changes.

How we would think about CLARION specifically

Most of what makes the platform trustworthy is not AI.

60%
Classical software
Instrument firmware, sample-handling protocols, calibration routines, data capture.
30%
Rule-based
Analyte identification thresholds, QC gates, site-specific correction factors.
10%
Genuine AI
VOC pattern classification, cross-site generalization, new-analyte discovery.

Teams who blur these layers ship slower and audit worse. Teams who separate them cleanly ship faster and pass audits without heroics. We design that separation, document it, and leave you with the framework.

The closest reference engagement

VigilOre. Multi-agent compliance, DRC mining.

We collapsed over 160 hours of manual regulatory compliance work to under five minutes by building an interpretable context layer above their existing tools. Different industry, same shape: regulated environment, document-heavy evidence chain, multiple stakeholders who each need a different view of the same underlying facts.
